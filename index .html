Soumita Roy

Economist · PhD Researcher
ETH Zürich · KOF Swiss Economic Institute

I'm a doctoral researcher at ETH Zürich studying how innovation, digitalisation, and networks shape economic systems. I work with large datasets, econometrics, NLP, and machine learning to explore how ideas spread and how technology transforms firms and markets. I'm curious about the circular economy, research networks, and anything that connects data with real-world impact.

Before Zürich, I spent three wonderful years in Geneva — working at IATA, Cartier Philanthropy, and UNCTAD — and completed my Master's at the Geneva Graduate Institute. I grew up in Kolkata, India, where I studied economics at Jadavpur University. Outside of research, I'm a classically trained Kathak dancer and someone who will never say no to good food or a new hiking trail. You'll occasionally find me at the gym, on a tennis court, or in the pool — staying active is how I keep the research brain sharp.

Research

Working papers and publications will appear here as my PhD progresses.

I am in the early stages of my doctoral research at ETH Zürich's KOF Swiss Economic Institute, Division of Innovation Economics. My work explores how innovation, digitalisation, and networks shape economic structures — including topics like the circular economy, knowledge diffusion, and how digital transformation affects firm competitiveness. I use large-scale datasets, econometrics, NLP, and machine learning alongside traditional methods. Check back soon for updates on working papers.
