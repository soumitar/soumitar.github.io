Research

Working papers and publications will appear here as my PhD progresses.

I am in the early stages of my doctoral research at ETH Zürich's KOF Swiss Economic Institute, Division of Innovation Economics. My work sits at the intersection of innovation economics, digital transformation, and network analysis, built around a central question: how do innovation and digitalisation reshape economic structures? This leads me to explore why some firms thrive through digital transformation while others struggle, how knowledge travels through research networks, and what truly drives the shift toward a circular economy. Methodologically, I bridge traditional econometrics and computational approaches, combining causal inference with NLP and machine learning on large-scale datasets. Working papers are in progress, so check back soon for updates.
