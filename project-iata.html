PaxInsight: Passenger Experience Analytics

Interactive survey design, data analysis, and benchmarking dashboards for global airline customer experience.

April 2024 — March 2025 · Soumita Roy · Interactive Demo
International Air Transport Association (IATA), Geneva

Overview: Below is an interactive demonstration of the PaxInsight platform I worked on at IATA. Explore the survey design, see how sampling works, and interact with the real-time analytics dashboards that were delivered to airline clients.

The survey: passenger journey mapping

PaxInsight captures passenger feedback across 60+ attributes covering the entire travel journey. Below is a sample survey showing the structure and question types. In practice, branching logic adapts questions based on cabin class and route distance to keep response time under 5 minutes.

Sample survey (premium cabin, long-haul flight)

Flight details

SQ 006 · Singapore to London · Business Class

Flown: 14 Mar 2025

How satisfied are you with your booking experience?

How satisfied are you with check-in?

How satisfied are you with in-flight catering?

Overall, how likely to recommend? (0-10)

Key feature: Responses collected 24–48 hours post-flight, when experience is salient but passengers are no longer exhausted. Stratified random sampling ensures the respondent population mirrors the actual flight manifest by class and origin/destination.

Dashboard 2: drill-down by cabin class

Overall metrics hide critical variations. Business class passengers prioritise service consistency; economy passengers care about efficiency. The dashboard below shows how satisfaction varies by class for SQ on Transpacific routes.

Dashboard 4: 12-month trend tracking

PaxInsight's true power is in trend monitoring. Executives don't just want their score; they want to know if it's improving, stable, or declining. The chart below shows month-over-month satisfaction for SQ's flagship Transpacific route over a full year.

Interpretation: SQ's overall satisfaction is stable (4.10–4.18 range) with no statistically significant decline. Notice the July dip (4.11) coincides with peak summer load factor. This suggests capacity creep is beginning to erode experience. SQ should monitor closely and consider capacity adjustments if scores drop below 4.05.

Technical workflow: from data collection to insights

The pipeline I built and operated monthly for all clients:

Collection Survey responses via platform, QA checks for outliers and missing data.
Cleaning R pipeline: handle missing values, flag inconsistencies, apply response weights by segment.
Analysis Compute satisfaction by airline, route, cabin, journey stage. Perform trend decomposition and CI calculations.
Dashboard Power BI refresh with drill-down, benchmark overlays, export-to-PDF for presentations.
Reporting Monthly 8–12 page report. Ad-hoc analysis. Quarterly strategy reviews with clients.

The work bridges statistics (sampling, confidence intervals), data engineering (R, SQL, ETL), and business communication (turning a 4.1 score into actionable insight).

Impact and deliverables

Over 12 months, I managed three concurrent airline clients with annual fees of €100k–150k each. Key deliverables:

  • 12 monthly reports per client (36 total), each 8–12 pages with 3–4 new insights and trend commentary.
  • 3 custom Power BI dashboards with real-time refresh, drill-down, and 40+ visualisations each.
  • 10+ ad-hoc analyses answering client-specific questions on route performance and competitive positioning.
  • Product roadmap input: Documented three feature requests that fed into PaxInsight's 2026 release cycle.

Measurable outcomes: one client improved Transpacific check-in from 3.6 to 3.9 over nine months through operational changes informed by the data. Another used the dashboard to justify a £2.3m premium cabin refurbishment, backed by satisfaction trend analysis showing stagnation.
