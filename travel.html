travel.

"The world is a book, and those who do not travel read only one page." — Saint Augustine

Living in Switzerland has been great for travel. A short train ride or a cheap flight and you're in a completely different country. I've made the most of it. I usually plan trips around food, something outdoorsy, or both. Some of my best travel memories involve a cliff I probably shouldn't have jumped off, or a meal I still think about months later. Below is a map of the countries I've been to so far, and a few trips that stand out.

Trips that stuck with me

Iceland

Northern lights, glaciers, and volcanic landscapes

Iceland was unlike anywhere I've been. The landscape feels like it belongs on another planet: black sand beaches, waterfalls pouring off cliffs into nothing, and geysers that erupt while you're standing right there. I went during winter, which meant short days but the northern lights more than made up for it. Driving the south coast in near-zero visibility with the wind rattling the car was terrifying and brilliant in equal measure. The kind of trip that makes you feel very small and very alive.

Croatia

Adriatic coastline, old towns, and cliff jumping

Croatia is where the adrenaline-rush side of my travel personality really came out. Cliff jumping into the Adriatic off the rocks near Dubrovnik, kayaking along the old city walls at sunset, and eating fresh seafood at tiny harbour restaurants. Split was gorgeous too, walking through Diocletian's Palace in the evening when the tourists thin out and it becomes a living, breathing neighbourhood again. The whole coast feels like it was designed for long, slow summer days.

France

Three years in Geneva, weekends in Lyon, Annecy, and Paris

I lived on the French border for three years, so France wasn't really a trip, it was a regular escape. Lyon for the food (the bouchons there are unbeatable), Annecy for the lake and the mountains, Chamonix for the snow, and Paris whenever I needed a dose of museums and bakeries. Living that close meant I got past the tourist version of France pretty quickly. My favourite moments were the small ones: a Sunday morning at a market in a village whose name I've already forgotten, or a long lunch in a courtyard somewhere in Provence.

Switzerland

Home base, hiking trails, and alpine lakes

It feels odd to call Switzerland a "trip" since I live here, but I've explored it more than most Swiss people I know. The hiking is the obvious highlight: Oeschinen Lake, the trails around Lauterbrunnen, the Rigi, the Aletsch Glacier. What surprised me was how different each region feels. The Italian-speaking Ticino is a world away from German-speaking Zürich, and both feel nothing like Geneva. I've also become a regular on the Zürich to Lucerne golden pass route, which never gets old. If you visit Switzerland, skip the chocolate shops and go straight to the mountains.

Add hero image: images/travel-greece-1.jpg

Greece

Island hopping, sunsets, and far too much feta

Greece was the trip where I did the least planning and had the most fun. I hopped between a few islands, spent entire days doing nothing but swimming and eating, and watched sunsets that genuinely made me put my phone down. The food is simple but perfect: grilled fish, fresh salads, bread with olive oil. Athens surprised me too. I expected ruins and chaos and got both, plus incredible street food and rooftop bars with views of the Acropolis lit up at night. It's the kind of place you go to once and immediately start planning your return.
